# Positioning

## Objective
Define the core message of the site.

## Questions
1. Who do you help?
2. What result do you deliver?
3. What makes you different?

## Output
Positioning Statement:
Eric helps homeowners who want to sell by guiding them to the best-fit path for their situation, whether that means listing the home on the market or receiving a direct purchase offer. He also helps buyers purchase a home, but the primary website message should focus on helping sellers who need clarity, support, and a realistic plan.

## Core Audience
- Homeowners who want to sell
- Homeowners with a specific motivation or deadline
- Homeowners who may need either a traditional listing path or a direct purchase option
- Secondary audience: buyers looking for help purchasing a home

## Desired Result
- Help sellers understand their options
- Match the right solution to their motivation, timeline, and price goals
- Give homeowners a clear next step instead of a one-size-fits-all sales pitch

## Differentiators
- Eric looks closely at each seller's motivation
- Eric considers the seller's timeline before recommending a path
- Eric evaluates pricing realistically to determine how he can best help
- Eric's approach is centered on helping people, not forcing the same solution on everyone

## Messaging Direction
- Lead with seller help and problem-solving
- Position Eric as a guide who helps homeowners figure out the best next move
- Emphasize that sellers may have more than one path forward
- Keep the tone practical, supportive, and trust-building
- Treat buyer services as a secondary message in navigation or supporting sections

## Handoff for Earl
Use messaging that frames Eric as a helpful, solutions-first real estate professional for sellers.

Key homepage angle:
- "Get clarity on your best way to sell"
- "Explore whether listing or a direct sale makes more sense for your situation"
- "Work with someone who looks at your motivation, timing, and goals before recommending a path"
